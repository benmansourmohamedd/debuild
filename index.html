About

This site is a CV-style index of work that treats architecture as infrastructure: a system that governs everyday life through policies, spatial rules, maintenance, and constraint. The emphasis is on methods, case specificity, and building research objects that remain usable over time.

Themes include shelter and institutional housing, carceral-adjacent environments, public space controls, and decolonial building science—how communities adapt, repair, and inhabit under structural precarity.
