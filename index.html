Method / About deBUILD

deBUILD is a research database documenting architecture as infrastructure: how spatial systems shape governance through access rules, control, maintenance, and constraint. The archive is designed for continuity and referenceability rather than visibility.

Design rules

Index-first navigation. Text-forward. Repeatable structure. Minimal decoration.

No portfolio hero. No personal homepage. The archive is the main object.

Geographic specificity

Cases prioritize comparability within a region (DC–Baltimore) while remaining legible as a general method.

Metadata is treated as part of the research object, not admin overhead.

Field protocol

Observe: thresholds, queues, signage, sightlines, barriers, staff-only boundaries, maintenance traces.

Record: time windows, eligibility scripting, “soft denial” mechanics, and where friction accumulates.

Entry template

Method Notes → Main Analysis → Diagrams/Images → References.

The goal is a stable unit of reference that can be revisited, compared, and expanded over time.
