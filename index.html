Method / About deBUILD

deBUILD is a research database documenting architecture as infrastructure: how spatial systems shape governance through access rules, control, maintenance, and constraint. The archive is designed for continuity and referenceability rather than visibility.

Scope

Housing insecurity, institutional environments, carceral-adjacent spaces, and public infrastructure.

Focus on geographic specificity and repeatable observation.

Entry format

Each entry uses the same sections so cases can be compared over time.

Field notes + diagrams + references; minimal narrative and no portfolio framing.

Method notes

Record thresholds, queues, signage, surveillance, maintenance traces, and enforcement patterns.

Store metadata (date/time, site type, access conditions) to stabilize the archive.

Design rules

Index-first navigation. No hero images. Text-forward. Repeatable structure. Quiet UI.

The archive is the main object; the editor page is secondary and contextual.

003 — Informal Repair Practices

DC–Baltimore Region • 2025 • Maintenance Culture
Method notes

Collect repair traces: patches, shims, taped seams, re-used parts, handwritten instructions.

Interview/situate “maintenance knowledge” as expertise: who fixes, with what tools, under what constraints.

Main analysis

Repair is a building science under precarity: adaptation becomes a survival infrastructure and a knowledge system.

Informal practices create continuity where formal systems fail or refuse maintenance.

006 — Waiting Rooms as Eligibility Filters

Montgomery County, MD • 2023 • Civic Services
Method notes

Record appointment systems: tickets, digital kiosks, timing, seating capacity, visibility to staff.

Observe “soft denial”: long waits, unclear signage, inaccessible forms, and forced exposure.

Main analysis

Waiting rooms function as procedural filters: time becomes a resource barrier and a compliance test.

Architecture distributes endurance unevenly—governing by friction rather than direct refusal.
