Shelter Is Not Housing

Date March 2026
Location DC–Baltimore Region
Type Spatial analysis / housing infrastructure
Focus Emergency shelter intake, circulation, surveillance, and governance

This entry examines emergency shelters as architectures of control, analyzing how intake procedures, circulation paths, and spatial surveillance reproduce carceral logics within systems of care.

The spatial organization of shelters often prioritizes monitoring, compliance, and throughput over privacy, autonomy, and dignity. These design decisions are not neutral; they encode policy, risk management, and moral judgment into built form.
