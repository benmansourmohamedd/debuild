Method / About deBUILD

deBUILD is Mohamed Benmansour’s index-first research archive for studying architecture as a socio-technical system: how governance and power become spatial rules, and how those rules can be modeled, visualized, and challenged through computation. The archive integrates algorithms, ethnomathematics, anthropology, and sociology through a decolonial lens.

Design rules

Index-first navigation. Text-forward. Repeatable structure. Minimal decoration.

No portfolio hero. No lifestyle framing. The archive is the main object.

What “computation” means here

Algorithms are treated as analytic tools and cultural artifacts—not neutral instruments.

Models and diagrams translate social procedures into spatial logics that can be compared across sites.

Field protocol

Observe: thresholds, queues, signage, sightlines, barriers, staff-only boundaries, maintenance traces.

Record: procedural scripting, “soft denial” mechanics, and where friction accumulates over time.

Ethnomathematics + social reading

Treat spatial reasoning as plural: measure how different practices encode logic, care, constraint, and survival.

Read built form as social organization: a meeting point of lived experience, institutional systems, and design.

Entry template

Method Notes → Main Analysis → Diagrams/Images → References.

Each entry is a stable unit for comparison: a repeatable method, not a one-off narrative.

Decolonial lens

Question what gets treated as “universal” in design and mathematics.

Center excluded knowledge systems, and track how institutions produce space as governance.
